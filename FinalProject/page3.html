Tim Berners-Lee

Tim Berners-Lee, born on June 8, 1955, is a British computer scientist best known as the inventor of the World Wide Web. He proposed the idea of an information management system that later became the foundation for the web.

Berners-Lee implemented the first successful communication between a Hypertext Transfer Protocol (HTTP) client and server via the internet in 1989. This marked the birth of the World Wide Web, revolutionizing the way information is shared and accessed globally.

In 1994, he founded the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) to oversee the development of web standards.

Tim Berners-Lee's contributions have had a profound impact on communication, collaboration, and the dissemination of information, shaping the modern internet as we know it.
