Vint Cerf and Bob Kahn

Vinton Cerf and Robert Kahn are American computer scientists who played a fundamental role in the development of the Internet. They are often referred to as the "Fathers of the Internet."

In the 1970s, Cerf and Kahn co-designed the Transmission Control Protocol (TCP) and the Internet Protocol (IP), the essential protocols that form the backbone of the Internet's communication system.

Their work laid the groundwork for the modern Internet, enabling the interconnected and global network of computers we use today.
