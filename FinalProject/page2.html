Steve Jobs



Steve Jobs, born on February 24, 1955, was an American business magnate, industrial designer, and inventor. He co-founded Apple Inc. and played a pivotal role in shaping the personal computer industry.

Jobs, along with Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne, founded Apple in 1976. The company's first product, the Apple I, was a single-board computer developed by Wozniak.

Throughout his career, Jobs was known for his visionary leadership and commitment to design excellence. He returned to Apple in 1997 and introduced innovative products like the iMac, iPod, iPhone, and iPad.
