Linus Torvalds

Linus Torvalds, born on December 28, 1969, is a Finnish-American software engineer best known as the creator of the Linux kernel.

In 1991, while a student at the University of Helsinki, Torvalds developed the first version of the Linux kernel and released it as free and open-source software.

Linux has since become a prominent example of free and open-source software development and is widely used in server environments and embedded systems.

Torvalds continues to oversee the development of the Linux kernel and remains an influential figure in the open-source community.
